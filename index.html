One scientist introduces the GeoStabilizer, a device to prevent land subsidence, while another creates HydroPure, a machine to convert seawater into clean water. However, a new problem arises: the scientists are demanding full patent rights. If granted, this life-saving technology will be costly, leaving the residents in dire straits. The dying city risks falling into despair again, this time not because of water, but because of greed.
Aruna protested fiercely in the council hall: "A city's survival should not be sold. Technology should serve humanity, not enslave it." Her voice shook the debate, pushing the council to reconsider their stance.

To avoid chaos, the city council reached a compromise. Code
By 2050, Jantara has transformed into a new, more advanced metropolis than ever before. Its citizens have become more environmentally conscious: waste is recycled into renewable energy, seawater is treated to produce clean water, and society is no longer repeating the mistakes of the past. The city survives not only because of technology, but also because society and science have finally learned to work hand in hand.
