Jatara Evolves: From a Sinking City to a Smart City

Orientation

In 2040, Jantara City—a once-thriving metropolis and center of government—is threatened with sinking due to rising sea levels and subsidence, also caused by climate change, exacerbated by melting polar ice, causing sea levels to rise drastically. The disaster predicted to sink the city in 2050 occurred a decade earlier. Heavy rains poured down non-stop for six straight months, turning the bustling city into a waterlogged ghost town.

Complications

The flood paralyzed every sector: transportation, electricity, education, and the economy. Jantara was grim-faced, silent, and... drowning. In desperation, the city government announced a major competition: any scientist who could save Jantara would be rewarded handsomely. Brilliant minds from around the world gathered, bringing innovative ideas.

Evaluation

One scientist introduces the GeoStabilizer, a device to prevent land subsidence, while another creates HydroPure, a machine to convert seawater into clean water. However, a new problem arises: the scientists are demanding full patent rights. If granted, this life-saving technology will be costly, leaving the residents in dire straits. The dying city risks falling into despair again, this time not because of water, but because of greed.

Resolution

To avoid chaos, the city council reached a compromise. The scientists were granted a futuristic, exclusive zone as their own, complete with luxury, security, and the freedom to continue their research. In return, the GeoStabilizer and HydroPure technologies were released for public use at no extra cost. This balance allowed the city to breathe a sigh of relief—its citizens were relieved, and the scientists remained respected.

Code

By 2050, Jantara has transformed into a new, more advanced metropolis than ever before. Its citizens have become more environmentally conscious: waste is recycled into renewable energy, seawater is treated to produce clean water, and society is no longer repeating the mistakes of the past. The city survives not only because of technology, but also because society and science have finally learned to work hand in hand.
